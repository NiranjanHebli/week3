## Part B:- Stretch Problem 

The goal of this assignment is to understand Boyce-Codd Normal Form (BCNF) and its differences from Third Normal Form (3NF). We will identify a schema that satisfies 3NF but violates BCNF and then perform decomposition to eliminate the violation. BCNF is a more rigorous normalization form than 3NF, and it is used to remove redundancy caused by functional dependencies where the determinants are not superkeys.

## Key Learning:-

- BCNF is stricter than 3NF

- Every BCNF relation is in 3NF, but not every 3NF relation is in BCNF.

- Functional dependency determines normalization quality

- 3NF may still allow redundancy

- BCNF decomposition improves consistency
